Common French Phrases

Bonjour

/bɔ̃.ʒuʁ/

A common greeting meaning "hello" or "good day". Used during the day until evening.

"Bonjour, comment allez-vous?" (Hello, how are you?)

Greetings

Au revoir

/o ʁə.vwaʁ/

A formal way to say "goodbye". Literally means "until we see each other again".

"Au revoir, à bientôt!" (Goodbye, see you soon!)

Greetings

S'il vous plaît

/sil vu plɛ/

The formal way to say "please". Used in polite or formal situations.

"Un café, s'il vous plaît." (A coffee, please.)

Politeness

Comment ça va?

/kɔ.mɑ̃ sa va/

Informal way to ask "how are you?" or "how's it going?"

"Salut! Comment ça va aujourd'hui?" (Hi! How are you today?)

Greetings

Enchanté(e)

/ɑ̃.ʃɑ̃.te/

"Nice to meet you" or "delighted" - used when meeting someone for the first time.

"Bonjour, je m'appelle Marie. - Enchantée!"

Greetings

Excusez-moi

/ɛk.sky.ze mwa/

Formal way to say "excuse me" or "I'm sorry".

"Excusez-moi, où est la gare?" (Excuse me, where is the train station?)

Politeness

Je ne comprends pas

/ʒə nə kɔ̃.pʁɑ̃ pa/

"I don't understand" - useful phrase when learning French.

"Je ne comprends pas, pouvez-vous répéter?"

Communication

Parlez-vous anglais?

/paʁ.le vu ɑ̃.ɡlɛ/

"Do you speak English?" - helpful when you need to switch languages.

"Excusez-moi, parlez-vous anglais?"

Communication

À bientôt

/a bjɛ̃.to/

"See you soon" - casual way to say goodbye.

"Merci pour votre aide, à bientôt!"

Greetings

Comment allez-vous?

/kɔ.mɑ̃ ta.le vu/

Formal way to ask "how are you?"

"Bonjour, comment allez-vous aujourd'hui?"

Greetings

Je m'appelle

/ʒə ma.pɛl/

"My name is" - used when introducing yourself.

"Bonjour, je m'appelle Sophie."

Introduction

Où est...?

/u ɛ/

"Where is...?" - used to ask for directions.

"Où est la gare la plus proche?"

Directions

Combien ça coûte?

/kɔ̃.bjɛ̃ sa kut/

"How much does it cost?" - asking about price.

"Cette robe est jolie, combien ça coûte?"

Shopping

Je voudrais

/ʒə vu.dʁɛ/

"I would like" - polite way to make a request.

"Je voudrais un café, s'il vous plaît."

Politeness

Quelle heure est-il?

/kɛl œʁ ɛ.til/

"What time is it?" - asking for the time.

"Excusez-moi, quelle heure est-il?"

Time

Je ne sais pas

/ʒə nə sɛ pa/

"I don't know" - expressing uncertainty.

"Désolé, je ne sais pas où c'est."

Communication

Pouvez-vous m'aider?

/pu.ve vu mɛ.de/

"Can you help me?" - asking for assistance.

"Excusez-moi, pouvez-vous m'aider?"

Help

Je suis désolé(e)

/ʒə sɥi de.zɔ.le/

"I'm sorry" - apologizing.

"Je suis désolé pour le retard."

Politeness

Merci beaucoup

/mɛʁ.si bo.ku/

"Thank you very much" - expressing gratitude.

"Merci beaucoup pour votre aide!"

Politeness

De rien

/də ʁjɛ̃/

"You're welcome" - responding to thanks.

"Merci! - De rien!"

Politeness

/ʒə sɥi pɛʁ.dy/

"I'm lost" - when you need directions.

"Excusez-moi, je suis perdu. Où est la gare?"

Directions

Immigration Terms

TEF Canada

/tef ka.na.da/

Test d'Évaluation de Français Canada - The official French language test for Canadian immigration.

"J'ai passé le TEF Canada pour mon dossier d'immigration."

Immigration

/se.ɛʁ.ɛs/

"Mon score CRS a augmenté grâce à mes compétences en français."

"J'ai atteint le niveau NCLC 7 en français."

Grammar Terms

Subjonctif

/syb.ʒɔ̃k.tif/

The subjunctive mood - used to express doubt, desire, or uncertainty.

"Il faut que je fasse mes devoirs." (I must do my homework.)

Grammar

Accord

/a.kɔʁ/

Agreement - the matching of gender and number between words.

"Les belles fleurs" (The beautiful flowers)

Grammar

Pronom

/pʁɔ.nɔ̃/

Pronoun - a word that replaces a noun in a sentence.

"Je, tu, il, elle, nous, vous, ils, elles"

Grammar

Conjugaison

/kɔ̃.ʒy.ɡɛ.zjɔ̃/

Verb conjugation - changing the form of a verb to match its subject and tense.

"Je mange, tu manges, il/elle mange" (I eat, you eat, he/she eats)

Grammar

Genre

/ʒɑ̃ʁ/

Gender - French nouns are either masculine or feminine.

"Le livre (masculine), la table (feminine)"

Grammar

Article

/aʁ.tikl/

Article - words like 'le', 'la', 'les' (the) and 'un', 'une', 'des' (a/some).

"Le chat, la maison, les enfants"

Grammar

Adjectif

/ad.ʒɛk.tif/

Adjective - word that describes a noun.

"Grand, petit, beau, belle" (Big, small, beautiful)

Grammar

Verbe

/vɛʁb/

Verb - action word in a sentence.

"Manger, dormir, parler" (To eat, to sleep, to speak)

Grammar

Temps

/tɑ̃/

Tense - indicates when an action takes place.

"Présent, passé, futur" (Present, past, future)

Grammar

Préposition

/pʁe.po.zi.sjɔ̃/

Preposition - word that shows relationship between words.

"à, de, dans, sur, sous" (to, of, in, on, under)

Grammar

Adverbe

/ad.vɛʁb/

Adverb - word that modifies a verb, adjective, or other adverb.

"rapidement, lentement, bien, mal" (quickly, slowly, well, badly)

Grammar

Conjonction

/kɔ̃.ʒɔ̃k.sjɔ̃/

Conjunction - word that connects words or clauses.

"et, ou, mais, car" (and, or, but, because)

Grammar

Participe

/paʁ.ti.sip/

Participle - verb form used as an adjective.

"mangé, fini, vu" (eaten, finished, seen)

Grammar

Infinitif

/ɛ̃.fi.ni.tif/

Infinitive - basic form of a verb.

"manger, dormir, parler" (to eat, to sleep, to speak)

Grammar

Conditionnel

/kɔ̃.di.sjɔ.nɛl/

Conditional - verb mood for hypothetical situations.

"Je mangerais, tu dormirais" (I would eat, you would sleep)

Grammar

Imparfait

/ɛ̃.paʁ.fɛ/

Imperfect - past tense for ongoing or habitual actions.

"Je mangeais, tu dormais" (I was eating, you were sleeping)

Grammar

Passé composé

/pa.se kɔ̃.po.ze/

Compound past - past tense for completed actions.

"J'ai mangé, tu as dormi" (I ate, you slept)

Grammar

Futur simple

/fy.tyʁ sɛ̃pl/

Simple future - tense for future actions.

"Je mangerai, tu dormiras" (I will eat, you will sleep)

Grammar

Plus-que-parfait

/plys kə paʁ.fɛ/

Pluperfect - past tense for actions before other past actions.

"J'avais mangé, tu avais dormi" (I had eaten, you had slept)

Grammar

Pronom relatif

/pʁɔ.nɔ̃ ʁə.la.tif/

Relative pronoun - connects clauses.

"qui, que, dont, où" (who, whom, whose, where)

Grammar

Pronom démonstratif

/pʁɔ.nɔ̃ de.mɔ̃s.tʁa.tif/

Demonstrative pronoun - points to specific things.

"celui, celle, ceux, celles" (this one, that one, these, those)

Grammar

Pronom possessif

/pʁɔ.nɔ̃ pɔ.sɛ.sif/

Possessive pronoun - shows ownership.

"le mien, la tienne, les nôtres" (mine, yours, ours)

Grammar

Pronom indéfini

/pʁɔ.nɔ̃ ɛ̃.de.fi.ni/

Indefinite pronoun - refers to unspecified things.

"quelqu'un, quelque chose, tout" (someone, something, everything)

Grammar

Pronom interrogatif

/pʁɔ.nɔ̃ ɛ̃.tɛ.ʁɔ.ga.tif/

Interrogative pronoun - used in questions.

"qui, que, quoi, lequel" (who, what, which)

Grammar
